EPHEMERIS FIELD SPECIFICATION — 3I/ATLAS

This document defines the ephemeris fields extracted from JPL Horizons
for positional residual and uncertainty analysis of the interstellar
object designated 3I/ATLAS.

The listed fields represent the minimum required set to:
• compare predicted vs observed astrometry
• evaluate motion consistency
• correctly assess positional uncertainty using the plane-of-sky error ellipse

All quantities are extracted directly from Horizons outputs without
numerical modification unless explicitly stated.

------------------------------------------------------------
REQUIRED FIELDS
------------------------------------------------------------

1. UTC_TIME
   Coordinated Universal Time of the ephemeris record.
   Format preserved exactly as returned by Horizons.

2. RA_ICRF
   Right Ascension in the International Celestial Reference Frame.
   Sexagesimal format: HH MM SS.sss

3. DEC_ICRF
   Declination in the International Celestial Reference Frame.
   Sexagesimal format: ±DD MM SS.ss

4. DRA_COSDEC
   Time derivative of Right Ascension multiplied by cos(DEC).
   Units: arcseconds per hour.
   Represents projected angular motion in RA.

5. DDEC_DT
   Time derivative of Declination.
   Units: arcseconds per hour.

------------------------------------------------------------
UNCERTAINTY AND ERROR ELLIPSE FIELDS
------------------------------------------------------------

6. RA_3SIGMA
   Formal ±3σ uncertainty in Right Ascension.
   Units: arcseconds.
   Represents orbit covariance uncertainty in the predicted position.
   NOT observational measurement error.

7. DEC_3SIGMA
   Formal ±3σ uncertainty in Declination.
   Units: arcseconds.
   Represents orbit covariance uncertainty in the predicted position.
   NOT observational measurement error.

8. SMAA_3SIG
   Semi-major axis of the 3σ plane-of-sky error ellipse.
   Units: arcseconds.
   Represents the direction of maximum positional uncertainty.

9. SMIA_3SIG
   Semi-minor axis of the 3σ plane-of-sky error ellipse.
   Units: arcseconds.
   Represents the direction of minimum positional uncertainty.

10. THETA
    Orientation angle of the error ellipse.
    Measured CLOCKWISE from the direction of increasing RA
    toward the semi-major axis, in the direction of increasing DEC.
    Units: degrees.

------------------------------------------------------------
INTERPRETATION NOTES
------------------------------------------------------------

• RA_3SIGMA and DEC_3SIGMA are projections of the orbit covariance and
  must NOT be treated as independent axis-aligned confidence bounds.

• The correct statistical comparison of residuals requires use of the
  rotated error ellipse defined by SMAA_3SIG, SMIA_3SIG, and THETA.

• Proper evaluation requires rotating the residual vector by THETA and
  testing whether it falls inside the normalized ellipse boundary.

• All uncertainty values assume Gaussian error propagation and may be
  optimistic if systematic biases are present.
